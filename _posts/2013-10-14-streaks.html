My name is Steve, and I used to be a binge coder.

I like the feeling of being on top of my life. I like to feel like I am getting always improving myself spiritually, mentally, physically, and socially. In college I joined a society of men who make that their motto. Once I started programming full-time, I found I loved working on side-projects, and I would feel guilty not advancing in my field in my free time.

But, I am a lazy man, and I would not do any extra work till my guilt would build up like a pressure cooker. Then one Saturday morning, I would binge code from like 6am till late in the evening. Looking back at my work after about 1pm, I would see a marked decline in actual value, but it would assuage my guilt for a few days.

Fast forward a few years, and GitHub comes out with their new "streak" feature on the front page.
