My name is Steve, and I used to be a binge coder.

When I first started programming full-time, I was hired with an embarassing lack of relavant skills. I felt guilty not advancing my skills in my free time, and that guilt, along with a bad case of imposter syndrome, stuck with me for a long time.

But, I am a lazy man. I would not do any extra work till my guilt built up like a pressure cooker. Usually, some Saturday morning I would binge code from like 6am till late in the evening. Looking back at my work after about 1pm, it was clearly worthless nonsense, but it would assuage my guilt for a few more days.

Fast forward a few years, and GitHub comes out with their new "streak" feature on the front page. If you look there now at anything from about six months ago, you will see the telltale signs of a binge coder: nothing for two weeks, then fifty commits in one weekend. I looked at that chart, and thought, "what if I tried to instead just do a little everyday?". This idea came over from my workout routine, where I had given up on sproadic and inconsistent workouts to a system of "workout every day, no matter how dumb".
